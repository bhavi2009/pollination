Pollination is the transfer of pollen grains from an anther of a plant to the stigma of a plant, later enabling fertilisation and the production of seeds, most often by an animal or by wind.

Self-pollination is a form of pollination in which pollen grains from the same plant arrives at the stigma of a flower or at the ovule.Cross-pollination is the process of applying pollen grains from one flower to the pistils of another flower. Pollination occurs in nature with the help of insects and wind.
